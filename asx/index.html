Key Takeaways for Capital Markets Participants:

Climate-Related Disclosures & Strategy

  • Second annual climate statement aligned with Aotearoa New Zealand Climate Standards.
  • Net Zero Commitment: Target to achieve net zero Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions by 2050, with interim science-aligned targets:
  • 54.6% reduction in absolute Scope 1 & 2 emissions by 2033 (2023 baseline).
  • 61% reduction in emissions intensity (per $1M revenue) by 2033.
  • Scope 3 Emissions: 98% of FY25 total emissions (11,416 tCO2e, down 53% YoY), excluding "use of sold products" (immaterial per adoption provisions).
  • Transition Plan: Focus on decarbonizing operations, customer enablement (e.g., fuel efficiency tools), and supply chain resilience.

Operational Highlights

  • FY25 Achievements:
  • Freight/logistics: Shifted to rail (NZ) and bulk shipments; reduced air freight reliance.
  • Circular economy: 100% hardware recycling (global rollout vs. NZ-only in FY24).
  • Fleet/energy: Downsized fleet vehicles, migrated to EVs, and switched NZ electricity to 100% renewable (Ecotricity).
  • Customer Impact:
  • Refrigerated fleets using EROAD tools achieved 65% pre-cooling time reduction and 50% fewer temperature faults.
  • AI dashcams ("Clarity Edge") improved safety, reducing fuel waste from incidents.

Financial & Risk Management

  • FY25 Spend:
  • $0.2M on emissions tools (Watershed), advisory (PwC), and assurance (Toitū).
  • $1.6M invested in FY24 for NZ Sustainability Module (1,005 active accounts by FY25).
  • Risks:
  • Transition: Policy/tech shifts (e.g., EV adoption), competition in emissions tracking.
  • Physical: Supply chain disruptions from climate events (low material impact to date).
  • No material climate risks identified in FY25; integrated into enterprise risk framework.

Governance & Metrics

  • Board/FRAC oversight; Sustainability Committee drives initiatives.
  • Assurance: Toitū provided reasonable assurance for Scope 1–2, limited assurance for Scope 3.
  • Key Metrics:
  • Emissions intensity: 58.72 tCO2e per $1M revenue (down 60% vs. FY23).
  • Contracted units: 255,845 (up 13% vs. FY23).

Conclusion: Progress on decarbonization and customer solutions, but Scope 3 remains a focus. No immediate trading impact; monitor execution against 2033 targets and EV/telematics competition.
